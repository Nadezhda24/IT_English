Definition

Due to peculiarities of programming languages syntax you can't use blank symbols in names of:

  • Variables
  • Functions
  • Classes

Instead of that you need to compound several words into one identificator like so: dontnamevariablesinthisway. Have you noticed that this is hard to read? It's so, but what can we do?

Naming styles

There're two popular(and possible) styles of naming everything in your code:

  1. Camel case. It was named after camel's hump - every new word have to begin with a capital letter. For example: totalCustomerCounter
  2. Snake case. It was named after snake body - underscores are put between every word pair. For instance: query_string

Usually software developers choose only one style and use it in all his projects for the sake bringing code into one style.

Analogue of camel case in Russian: верблюжий стиль.

Analogue of snake case in Russian: змеиный стиль.
